# Blog Writing Style Guide — All Clients

## Voice & Tone
- Write directly TO the reader (second person — "you", "your", not "homeowners" or "investors")
- Conversational but authoritative — like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook
- Add flair — make it interesting, not dry corporate content
- Short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones for rhythm
- Use "you" constantly — the reader should feel like you're talking specifically to them

## Structure
- Meta title + meta description (SEO)
- Blog overview (3-5 bullet points)
- Introduction that hooks immediately — no slow warm-ups
- H2/H3 headings throughout
- Blockquote callouts for key insights/tips
- Local suburb/location keywords woven naturally throughout
- 7-10 FAQ questions at the end
- Strong CTA closing paragraph

## What to Avoid
- Third person ("homeowners", "investors", "buyers" — replace with "you")
- Boring intros that state the obvious
- Overly formal language
- Fluff without substance
- Generic advice that could apply anywhere — make it LOCAL

## Topic Research
- Research trending topics in the client's industry before writing
- Look at what's ranking, what's being searched, what's in the news
- Blend trending topics with the client's specific services and locations
- For Quantum: always tie back to Brisbane suburbs, the buying process, and strategy-first approach
- For service businesses: tie back to local suburbs, specific problems, seasonal relevance

## Examples of Good Opening Lines
BAD: "Bathroom renovations are an important investment for homeowners."
GOOD: "Your bathroom is probably the one room in your house that needs to work hard every single day — and in 2026, it's time to make it work beautifully too."

BAD: "A buyer's agent can help you purchase property in Brisbane."
GOOD: "Here's the honest truth: buying property in Brisbane without a buyer's agent in today's market is like navigating a city you've never visited without GPS."
