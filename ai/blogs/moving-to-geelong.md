# Moving to Geelong: Why It’s Australia’s Top New Hotspot

Moving to Geelong has never been more appealing—and now the data proves it. This thriving Victorian city has officially become the most popular relocation destination in Australia. As a result, thousands of Australians are now looking to Geelong as a place to live, invest, and grow.

Once considered a quiet industrial centre, Geelong has evolved into a dynamic city that offers a rare combination of affordability, lifestyle, and opportunity. Therefore, it comes as no surprise that people from all over the country are packing up and relocating.

## Why Are So Many People Moving to Geelong?

Here’s why more Australians are choosing moving to Geelong over staying in the capital cities:

- **Affordability**: Compared to Melbourne, house prices in Geelong are significantly lower. Consequently, buyers can get far more value for their money.
- **Proximity to Melbourne**: Geelong is only about an hour from Melbourne, which means residents enjoy coastal living without losing access to the city.
- **Lifestyle**: The waterfront, local markets, cafes, and creative culture all contribute to a vibrant, relaxed lifestyle.
- **Job Opportunities**: Meanwhile, key industries such as healthcare, education, and construction are expanding, offering stable career paths.
- **Family-Friendly Living**: For example, top-tier schools and safe neighbourhoods make Geelong ideal for raising a family.

## Official Data on the Move

According to the Regional Movers Index, Geelong led the nation in internal migration growth during the first quarter of 2025. The report, released by the Commonwealth Bank and the Regional Australia Institute, highlighted that many Australians are moving for lifestyle benefits and housing affordability. As expected, moving to Geelong came out on top in both categories.

## Why Investors Are Also Moving to Geelong

Not only are families and professionals relocating to Geelong, but property investors are also paying close attention. For example, suburbs such as Armstrong Creek, Highton, and Charlemont are seeing strong rental demand and solid capital growth.
