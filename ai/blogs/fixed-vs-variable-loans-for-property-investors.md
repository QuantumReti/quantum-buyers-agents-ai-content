# Fixed vs Variable Loans for Property Investment

When it comes to financing your next property investment in Victoria, one of the most crucial decisions you’ll face is choosing between fixed vs variable loans. The choice can significantly impact your cash flow, investment strategy, and long-term wealth-building potential.

At Quantum Buyers Agents, we work with property investors every day, helping them make smart, strategic decisions tailored to their goals. In this article, we break down the pros and cons of fixed vs variable loans and explain how to make the right choice based on your investment objectives.

## Fixed vs Variable Loans Explained: Choosing Stability or Flexibility

A fixed rate loan locks in your interest rate for a set period—commonly 1 to 5 years.

### Pros

- **Predictable repayments**: Ideal for budgeting, especially if you’re risk-averse.
- **Protection from rate hikes**: If interest rates rise, your repayments stay the same.
- **Good for new investors**: Offers peace of mind during the early stages of your investment journey.

### Cons

- **Less flexibility**: Early exit often comes with costly break fees.
- **Limited features**: May restrict offset accounts or redraw facilities.
- **No benefit if rates fall**: You may end up paying more than necessary.

## Variable Loan Benefits: What Property Investors Should Know

A variable rate loan moves with the market. When the RBA adjusts rates, so do your repayments.

### Pros

- **Greater flexibility**: Usually includes offset accounts, redraw facilities, and the ability to make extra repayments.
- **Benefit from rate cuts**: You pay less when interest rates drop.
- **Easier to switch lenders**: No fixed term means fewer penalties if you want to refinance.

### Cons

- **Uncertainty**: Fluctuating repayments make budgeting more challenging.
- **Risk of rising rates**: Increases in interest rates can eat into your rental yield.

## What’s Better for Investors in Victoria: Fixed vs Variable Loans?

Deciding between fixed vs variable loans depends on your personal situation, investment goals, and risk tolerance.

- If you want certainty, are risk-averse, or are just starting out, a fixed loan may offer more peace of mind.
- If you’re looking for flexibility, have strong cash flow, or want to take advantage of market conditions, a variable loan could be a better fit.
- Many experienced investors opt for a split loan—a combination of both—to balance stability and flexibility.

## 2025 Market Outlook: How Fixed vs Variable Loans Compare

Victoria’s property market is stabilising after a turbulent rate cycle. With the Reserve Bank holding steady for now but leaving room for future changes, interest rates remain a key consideration. For investors, this makes choosing between fixed vs variable loans not just a financial decision, but a strategic one that impacts overall portfolio performance.
