# Melbourne Property Recovery: Signs of Market Rebound in 2025

After several years of uncertainty, the Melbourne property recovery is now gaining momentum as we move through 2025. Indeed, a combination of stabilising interest rates, increased buyer confidence, and sustained population growth is breathing new life into one of Australia’s most dynamic real estate markets.

## Key Drivers Behind the Melbourne Property Recovery in 2025

Several key factors are contributing to this encouraging Melbourne property recovery:

### 1. Interest Rate Stability Fuels Recovery

Following aggressive rate hikes in recent years, the Reserve Bank of Australia has adopted a more stable monetary policy. As a result, this shift has provided relief to homeowners and investors alike, reigniting demand and boosting confidence in the market.

### 2. Increased Buyer Activity

At Quantum Buyers Agents, we’ve noticed a clear uptick in buyer engagement. Moreover, owner-occupiers and investors are actively searching and bidding, with auctions becoming more competitive and off-market opportunities growing.

### 3. Supply Constraints Fueling Price Growth

The ongoing shortage of quality housing stock, especially in Melbourne’s middle-ring suburbs, is a significant driver of the current market recovery. Furthermore, construction delays and planning challenges have tightened supply, contributing to upward price pressure.

### 4. Strong Population Growth

Melbourne continues to attract interstate and international migrants. Meanwhile, with borders open and employment opportunities expanding, demand for housing remains high, particularly in suburbs with good infrastructure and lifestyle appeal.

## Suburbs Leading the Melbourne Property Recovery in 2025

Our team at Quantum Buyers Agents has identified several suburbs showing strong signs of recovery and growth potential:

- **Preston and Coburg**: Benefiting from ongoing gentrification and proximity to the CBD.
- **Werribee and Tarneit**: Attractive for first-home buyers and investors seeking value.
- **Bentleigh East and Moorabbin**: Established family-friendly suburbs with strong school zones.

In addition, these areas offer a blend of affordability and lifestyle amenities that are driving renewed interest.
