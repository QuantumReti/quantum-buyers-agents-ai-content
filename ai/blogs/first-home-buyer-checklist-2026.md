## 1. Get Your Finances in Order

Before you even think about house-hunting, it’s crucial to get your financial groundwork right. This defines your buying power and sets realistic expectations.

### Pre-Approval Checklist for First Home Buyers

Getting a loan pre-approval tells you exactly how much you can borrow and ensures sellers take your offers seriously. Prepare the following:

- Recent payslips and employment verification.
- Savings history and bank statements.
- Credit score and existing loan records.
- Proof of identification and residential status.

> **Tip from Quantum Buyers Agents:** Many buyers look for properties before securing pre-approval, which often leads to disappointment if financing falls short later.

### First Home Buyer Budget Checklist: Upfront Costs

Your budget should include more than just the deposit. Factor in:

- **Stamp Duty:** Some states offer first-home exemptions, but check current 2026 thresholds.
- **Legal/Conveyancing Fees:** Essential for contract reviews and title transfers.
- **Inspections:** Building and pest reports.
- **Lender Fees:** Loan establishment and valuation fees.
- **Ongoing Costs:** Pro-rata council rates and insurance.

## 2. Research & Shortlist Your Dream Suburb

Your location choice is more than just lifestyle; it’s an investment in your future.

### Suburbs Selection Australia 2026

Ask yourself:

- What is the potential for capital growth in this area?
- Are there upcoming infrastructure projects (rail, road, schools) nearby?
- How close is it to your workplace and transport hubs?
- What are the local crime rates and community vibes?

## 3. Inspect Before You Invest

Never skip inspections. What looks perfect on the outside might hide costly structural or maintenance nightmares.

### First Home Buyer Inspection Checklist

Before making an offer, always check:

- **Structural Soundness:** Walls, ceilings, and foundations.
- **Systems:** Plumbing, drainage, and electrical wiring.
- **The Roof:** Roofing, insulation, and signs of moisture/leaks.
- **Pests:** Evidence of termites (especially common in Queensland).

---

## 4. Understand the Contract

The contract stage is where many first-time buyers feel overwhelmed. Your contract should clearly outline:

- Property inclusions and exclusions (fixtures, appliances, etc.).
- Settlement date and deposit amount.
- **Subject to Finance** and **Subject to Building/Pest** clauses.
- Penalty conditions for withdrawal.

**Never sign without a conveyancer or buyer’s agent reviewing the document first.**

---

## 5. How Much Should You Offer?

This is where the art of negotiation comes in. Offer too high and you overpay; offer too low and you lose the property.

### Negotiation Tactics

- **Research:** Look at comparable recent sales in the immediate area.
- **Motivation:** Try to understand why the seller is moving (are they in a rush?).
- **Confidence:** Having your pre-approval ready makes you a "clean" buyer.
- **Emotion:** Stay calm and avoid getting caught in emotional bidding wars.

---

## 6. Avoid These Common Mistakes

- Falling in love with a property too fast and skipping due diligence.
- Ignoring "hidden" costs like strata fees or building insurance.
- Not checking a property’s zoning or flood risk.
- Missing critical deadlines for finance or settlement.
- Attempting to negotiate alone against seasoned selling agents.

---

## 7. Take Advantage of Government Grants & Schemes

Australia continues to offer multiple incentives for first-home buyers in 2026, though eligibility differs by state. Check if you qualify for:

- **First Home Owner Grant (FHOG):** Available for new builds in many states.
- **Stamp Duty Concessions:** Significant savings for first-timers under certain price caps.
- **Home Guarantee Scheme:** Allows you to buy with as little as a 5% deposit without Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI).
- **Energy Efficiency Incentives:** Grants for purchasing sustainable or high-energy-rated homes.

---

## 8. The Settlement Process Explained

Once your offer is accepted, the settlement period (typically 30–90 days) begins:

1. **Finalise Mortgage:** Your lender performs a final valuation and issues formal approval.
2. **Sign Documents:** Finalise legal paperwork with your conveyancer.
3. **Final Inspection:** Conduct a walk-through 1–3 days before settlement to ensure the property is in the agreed condition.
4. **Handover:** Funds are transferred, and you receive the keys.
